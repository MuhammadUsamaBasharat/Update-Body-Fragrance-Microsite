Behind the Incense
Our Incense Collection has been artisanally crafted by the Koh-Shi aroma masters on the Japanese island of Awaji. One of the earliest written records of Japanese history recounts that a large agar log washed ashore in the sixth century and emanated such a beautiful aroma when the villagers burned some of it as firewood that they gifted the rest to the prince and empress. Prince Shotoku commissioned a statue from it and asked the villagers to enshrine the remaining wood. The exquisite Kareiki Shrine still stands today.
The Awaji began making incense in the 1850s, when a trader saw the island’s potential, thanks not only to its bustling port, but also to the strong western winds that aid in drying the incense naturally. Over the centuries, trade associations were formed, and the Koh-Shi, or incense master, accreditation was established. Today, the Koh-Shis are famed for their ability to establish perfect harmony when crafting incense from raw materials that change every year due to weather and natural growing conditions.
Because of our dedication to craftsmanship at the highest level, we sought a partnership with one of Awaji’s most legendary incense makers, who have infused their scent sticks with our fragrances.

Behind the Fragrance Bottle
Oribe packaging is designed to create a visual statement that sparks an emotional reaction, becoming a covetable object on its own.The distinctive fragrance bottles are inspired by post-modernist art and architecture, featuring elongated oval shapes and a sculptural, one-of-a-kind resin cap whose color echoes the scent within.
Oribe partnered with a glassmaker located in Normandy, France that has specialized in producing fragrance glass bottles since 1892. The exceptional color and brightness of their glass is universally recognized as the highest quality possible for fragrance. Each Oribe bottle is hand-polished and inspected by the atelier’s master craftspeople, and is minimally decorated to allow the artisanal quality of the glass to shine through.
To complete the bottle design, a French manufacturer developed custom resin caps by combining both modern and Old World techniques. Each is unique in color, so no two will look exactly alike. The colors were created to evoke the fragrances: sandy tortoiseshell evocative of the beaches of the Côte d’Azur, tranquil green of the blooming desert for Desertland and marbled violet and rose for Valley of Flowers’ vibrant bouquet.
